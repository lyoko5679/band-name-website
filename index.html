Peanut Crisis

Peanut Crisis is a band that is, technically, a real band. Yes, the main instrument is peanut jars. Yes, they are shaken. And yes, it is really jazz. The group insists on this point often. What started as a joke quickly became something they take very seriously, even if it does not look like it. The idea is simple: treat peanut jars like instruments and commit fully. It is really jazz.

Each member has their own approach to the jar. Some focus on rhythm, some on texture, and some just shake harder, which is also valid. The amount of peanuts in each jar matters. The grip matters. The motion matters. It is all part of the sound. And again, it is really jazz.

There is improvisation, there is interaction, and there is the constant attempt to listen and respond to each other, just like any ensemble. Live performances tend to confuse people at first. It looks like a joke, but then patterns emerge, and suddenly it feels structured. The band leans into that moment. Peanut Crisis is committed to the idea that music can come from anywhere, even a jar of peanuts. It sounds ridiculous, but they will remind you: it is really jazz.
